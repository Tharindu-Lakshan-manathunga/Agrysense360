AgriSense360 Introduction

Introduction

AgriSense360 is an innovative precision agriculture system designed to support Sri Lankan farmers by combining traditional farming with advanced technology. It uses a distributed network of IoT sensors connected via LoRaWAN to monitor soil conditions (moisture, pH, nutrients), weather, and crop health in real time. Infrared imaging is integrated to detect early signs of crop stress and diseases without harming plants. The collected data is analyzed using machine learning models—linear regression, deep neural networks, and SVM for accurate crop yield prediction (78.34% accuracy) and disease detection in tomatoes (85% accuracy with CNN models). The system is cost-effective and flexible, making it accessible to smallholders and large agribusinesses alike. A mobile application dashboard delivers actionable insights such as soil health indices, crop water stress levels, and bacteria risk to farmers, enabling timely, data-driven decisions. AgriSense360 aims to enhance productivity, manage risks, and promote sustainable farming practices amid climate challenges and increasing food demand in Sri Lanka.

The field of mental health and AI research is active, with a focus on predicting stress levels [3]. Studies have explored the relationship between stress and physiological factors, such as heart rate variability, cortisol levels, and skin conductance. Wearable devices like smartwatches provide real-time physiological data for stress prediction. Various research papers have developed stress management questionnaires, including the DASS42 scale [3] for identifying depression, anxiety, and stress. Machine learning algorithms, specifically Support Vector Machine and Logistic Regression, achieved high accuracy in predicting these conditions, around 97% and 98%, respectively. Deep learning techniques have been explored for detecting users' mental states [4].

Self-reflection and journaling practices are recognized for enhancing self-awareness and emotional regulation. A study by Smith et al. [5] in 2019 highlighted the benefits of regular journaling in fostering personal growth, with effective features including free writing, guided prompts, and structured exercises. Natural language processing (NLP) systems like BioBERT and ClinicalBERT play a crucial role in understanding journal content and classifying entries by emotional tone. Johnson et al.'s research [6] in 2017 introduced a sentiment analysis system for tracking emotional changes through journal entry analysis, aiding individuals in monitoring their mental well-being and personal growth over time.

Positive thinking, emotions, and feelings play a vital role in improving mental health. DeMuse, a mood-based music app, stems from research exploring music's stress-reduction impact [7]. Nature sounds have proven effective for physiological recovery after stress. The Stress Reduction Theory and the Attention Restoration Theory underline nature's positive influence on emotions and focus [8]. This connection leads to therapies like guided imagery, mindfulness, and eco-therapy for mental well-being. Facial Emotion Recognition (FER) is explored in therapy, with a CNN-based approach achieving 80% accuracy [9]. Online therapy has been in practice since before 2016 [8].

In the field of text-based image generation, notable systems have emerged, each with distinctive features. DALL·E [10], developed by OpenAI, is a pioneering model that seamlessly blends GAN architecture with Transformer models to create images based on textual descriptions. For example, it can transform text prompts into vivid images, such as "a Male Lion walking in the jungle." Another remarkable system, AttnGAN [11], excels at generating highly detailed and coherent images from text descriptions. AttnGAN uses global and local attention mechanisms to refine the generated images, making it well-suited for creative applications, especially in art generation.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Stress Prediction

Researchers in the field have primarily focused on machine learning and IoT-based approaches to predict stress levels. However, a research gap exists regarding the most relevant predictive factors and their interactions. Many existing studies require costly and complex hardware, limiting accessibility. Moreover, they often lack comprehensive and multimodal feature incorporation. The proposed system streamlines data collection with the involvement of mental health experts and prioritizes user-centered design.

Approaches for Stress Reduction

Research reveals significant gaps in understanding the effectiveness of complementary health approaches, mindfulness interventions, and yoga in improving mental well-being, particularly in managing stress. Motivations, outcomes, and the efficacy of these interventions are inadequately addressed. Additionally, there's a need for meta-analyses in assessing the effects of Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) and Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT) on mental health.

Mindfulness and Mental Health Apps

The research identifies a gap in the accurate assessment of a user's mental health condition within existing mental health apps. To address this gap, it suggests leveraging methods like voice or emotion recognition, which could provide more precise insights into a user's emotional state. Additionally, the integration of facial recognition and AI technologies could offer a means to tailor therapy recommendations more personally, based on the individual's emotional and mental condition.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to mitigate challenges in addressing mental health disorders and improve mental well being with the utilization of advanced technologies ?

The COVID-19 pandemic has led to a significant increase in mental health issues due to factors like isolation, monotony, heavy workloads, and financial stress. Beyond the pandemic, the competitive and high-pressure modern world poses ongoing challenges to mental well-being. Many people lack access to advanced mental health solutions, and addressing mental health is inherently complex. Efforts to digitize mental health approaches are underway, but ensuring the effectiveness of these technology-driven methods remains a key challenge. This underscores the need for innovative approaches to bridge the gap between traditional and digital mental health solutions in an ever-evolving world.


Proposed Solution

The "Serenity" mobile application incorporates multiple elements to improve emotional well-being. It assesses stress levels through chat interactions, utilizing Natural Language Processing (NLP) with a Sequential neural network for sentiment analysis. Stress level prediction is carried out through a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) classifier. Additionally, the self-reflection and journaling feature employs NLP to predict emotions from text using Logistic Regression, subsequently suggesting mood-enhancing activities through content-based filtering. The application also makes use of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) with transfer learning for facial emotion recognition and provides video recommendations tailored to the user's emotions through content-based filtering. To add a personal touch, Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN) generate personalized visual content. Collectively, these diverse features offer users a comprehensive tool for managing stress and improving emotional well-being.

Research Objectives

Stress Prediction Through Deep Learning

The first objective is to create a deep learning model capable of accurately predicting an individual's stress levels using a range of factors, including environmental, behavioral, and biological variables. This model is intended to aid individuals and healthcare providers in the early identification and management of stress, thus preventing it from progressing into a chronic condition.

AI based self reflective journaling and activity suggestion

The second objective of this project is to develop an AI-powered self-reflection and journaling system that serves as a valuable tool for users to understand their thoughts and emotions. Furthermore, assists users in identifying and implementing effective strategies to manage their mental health effectively.

Facial Emotion Recognition & Video Recommendation

The third objective is to enhance the understanding of users by employing machine learning techniques for emotion recognition. This involves analyzing and identifying the emotional states of users as they interact with the application. Once the user's emotions are successfully recognized, the subsequent step is to provide personalized therapeutic videos that are specifically tailored to match the user's identified emotional state.

Generate custom images from user text input.

The fourth objective is to Offering users the ability to create calming images can be a powerful tool for stress reduction. This feature allows individuals to engage in a therapeutic and creative process, providing a welcome respite from the demands of everyday life. By generating soothing visuals, users can find relaxation and solace in the act of artistic expression. This not only serves as a means of self-care but also fosters a sense of accomplishment and well-being.

Methodology

Figure 3. High Level Architecture of the System.

The proposed system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Stress Prediction Through Deep Learning.
  2. AI based self reflective journaling and activity suggestion.
  3. Facial Emotion Recognition & Video Recommendation.
  4. Generate custom images from user text input.

The system diagram in Figure 3 outlines the proposed solution, which aims to provide a smart mobile application for reducing stress and uplifting people's emotions through AI integration. This application can be used by individuals with or without mental health disorders to monitor their emotions, stress levels, and access strategies for stress reduction and emotional well-being. It caters not only to mental health patients and professionals but also to the general public. The application has two key components: user understanding and mental health improvement. For user understanding, it employs stress prediction through chat conversations and emotion prediction using journal entries and facial expressions. To enhance mental health, the app offers mood-based stress reduction activities, therapy video recommendations, and personalized visual content generation based on user input.

Authenticated users can access the application's features, including starting a chat to assess their stress levels through NLP sentiment analysis and MLP classification.Users can also maintain journals, with emotions identified using NLP-based text-to-emotion analysis through Logistic Regression. Facial expressions are detected through the mobile selfie camera using CNN-based transfer learning models. Based on identified emotions, the app suggests stress-reducing activities and therapy videos using content-based filtering mechanisms. Users can also request personalized visual content through the chat, with GAN models utilized for visual generation. The machine learning models for sentiment analysis, stress prediction, journal-based emotion prediction, activity suggestions, facial emotion recognition, video recommendations, and visual content generation are deployed within the Flask server.
